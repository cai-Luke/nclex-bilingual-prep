# Case 1: Adult Preventive Screening and Health Literacy

Write an unfolding NCLEX-RN case study skeleton in English.

**Topic:** Health Promotion and Maintenance — Adult preventive screening, vaccination counseling, and health literacy.

**Scenario Frame:**
A middle-aged adult presents for a clinic visit after several years without consistent preventive care. The client has limited health literacy, competing work/family obligations, and uncertainty about why screening matters when they “feel fine.” The visit surfaces several overdue preventive-care needs, but the case should not turn into chronic disease management such as diabetes, hypertension, or lipid management.

**Objectives — what the case must test:**

* Distinguishing screening from diagnostic testing.
* Prioritizing preventive care when multiple screenings or vaccines are overdue.
* Using plain-language teaching.
* Assessing teach-back, including the possibility of partial or incomplete understanding.
* Engaging in nonjudgmental therapeutic communication to address embarrassment, fear, or avoidance.
* Planning follow-up around schedule, caregiver, transportation, or cost barriers.
* Recognizing when a finding needs timely follow-up versus routine screening.

# Case 2: Pediatric Anticipatory Guidance and Home Safety

Write an unfolding NCLEX-RN case study skeleton in English.

**Topic:** Health Promotion and Maintenance — Pediatric anticipatory guidance, developmental safety, and caregiver teaching.

**Scenario Frame:**
A caregiver brings an infant, toddler, or preschool-age child for a well-child visit or urgent clinic follow-up. During the visit, the nurse identifies home-safety and developmental-risk cues, such as unsafe sleep, car-seat misuse, choking hazards, medication storage, fall risk, burn risk, water safety, or unsafe over-the-counter medication use.

**Objectives — what the case must test:**

* Delivering age-appropriate safety teaching.
* Prioritizing the most immediate safety risk.
* Differentiating caregiver knowledge gaps from neglect.
* Using nonjudgmental caregiver education to correct unsafe practices.
* Reinforcing correct developmental expectations.
* Identifying when urgent clinical or safety escalation is warranted.
* Assessing caregiver teach-back.

# Case 3: Dementia Caregiver Role Strain

Write an unfolding NCLEX-RN case study skeleton in English.

**Topic:** Psychosocial Integrity — Caregiver role strain, dementia care, family coping, and safe home support.

**Scenario Frame:**
An older adult with dementia is being evaluated in a clinic, emergency department, or home-health setting. The family caregiver is exhausted, missing work, losing sleep, and using unsafe or borderline unsafe strategies to manage wandering, agitation, toileting, medications, feeding, or nighttime supervision.

**Objectives — what the case must test:**

* Recognizing caregiver role strain.
* Distinguishing overwhelmed-caregiver support needs from abuse or neglect red flags.
* Using nonjudgmental therapeutic communication with the caregiver.
* Developing a safety plan for wandering, falls, medications, nutrition, toileting, and supervision.
* Referring to respite care, social work, adult day programs, home support, or caregiver support groups.
* Prioritizing immediate safety risks without placing blame on the caregiver.
* Evaluating the safety and sustainability of the home care plan.

# Case 4: Substance Use Recovery and Relapse Risk

Write an unfolding NCLEX-RN case study skeleton in English.

**Topic:** Psychosocial Integrity — Substance use recovery, relapse risk, harm reduction, safety assessment, and therapeutic communication.

**Scenario Frame:**
A client in early recovery from alcohol or opioid use disorder is seen in an outpatient, behavioral health, emergency department, or medical-surgical setting. The client faces relapse triggers, shame, family conflict, sleep disruption, pain, medication questions, fear of judgment, or unstable follow-up.

**Objectives — what the case must test:**

* Using nonjudgmental therapeutic communication in response to emotionally loaded client statements.
* Recognizing relapse-risk cues.
* Differentiating cravings, withdrawal symptoms, overdose risk, and suicidal ideation.
* Delivering harm-reduction teaching when relevant, such as naloxone use for opioid overdose risk or medication-assisted treatment support when included in the scenario.
* Supporting medication adherence and recovery resources.
* Identifying when safety assessment or urgent escalation is required.

# Case 5: Comfort, Mobility, Toileting, and Pressure Injury Prevention

Write an unfolding NCLEX-RN case study skeleton in English.

**Topic:** Basic Care and Comfort — Comfort, mobility, toileting, fall prevention, nutrition/hydration support, and pressure injury prevention.

**Scenario Frame:**
A frail older adult, post-op client, or rehab client has pain, limited mobility, urinary urgency or incontinence, poor intake, sleep disruption, and early skin-integrity risk. The scenario focuses on practical nursing care rather than acute surgical complication diagnosis.

**Objectives — what the case must test:**

* Balancing pain control with safe mobilization.
* Preventing pressure injury through repositioning, offloading, and skin/moisture care.
* Coordinating safe toileting and fall prevention.
* Supporting nutrition, hydration, hygiene, sleep, and comfort.
* Recognizing early skin breakdown.
* Evaluating the effectiveness of basic care and mobility interventions.

# Case 6: Infection Control in a Realistic Care Cluster

Write an unfolding NCLEX-RN case study skeleton in English.

**Topic:** Safety and Infection Control — Standard precautions, transmission-based precautions, visitor teaching, shared equipment, and cross-contamination prevention.

**Scenario Frame:**
A nurse cares for multiple clients with different infection-control needs during a busy shift. The scenario involves clustered care, shared equipment, family visitors, environmental cleaning, PPE selection/removal, hand hygiene, and at least one observed isolation breach.

**Objectives — what the case must test:**

* Selecting appropriate PPE based on the clinical situation.
* Recognizing isolation breaches.
* Prioritizing the breach that creates the greatest transmission risk.
* Dedicating or disinfecting shared equipment.
* Teaching visitors about isolation precautions nonjudgmentally.
* Sequencing care tasks to reduce cross-contamination.
* Evaluating the effectiveness of infection-control interventions.
